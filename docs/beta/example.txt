Recommended Contract Redlines Specific language to propose during renegotiation Section 5.1 — Rebate Passthrough HIGH IMPACT Current Language (Remove) "PBM shall pass through to Plan Sponsor no less than eighty-five percent (85%) of all Rebates received by PBM from pharmaceutical manufacturers that are attributable to the utilization of covered drugs by Members under this Agreement." Suggested Language (Add) "PBM shall pass through to Plan Sponsor one hundred percent (100%) of all compensation received by PBM from pharmaceutical manufacturers, including but not limited to base rebates, administrative fees, volume bonuses, price protection payments, market share incentives, data fees, and service fees, regardless of how such payments are characterized by the manufacturer or PBM." Why: The current language allows PBM to keep 15% and leaves room to exclude other manufacturer payments from the rebate pool. Expanding the definition and requiring 100% passthrough prevents retained manufacturer compensation from becoming hidden PBM margin. Source: Gold-standard state PBM contract language or ERISA best practice Section 4.5 — Spread Pricing Disclosure HIGH IMPACT Current Language (Remove) "Plan Sponsor acknowledges and agrees that PBM may retain the difference ('Spread') between the amount PBM charges Plan Sponsor for a dispensed prescription and the amount PBM reimburses the dispensing pharmacy. PBM is under no obligation to disclose the Spread or the amounts reimbursed to individual pharmacies for individual claims. The Spread shall constitute additional compensation to PBM and is reflected in the guaranteed pricing terms set forth herein." Suggested Language (Add) "PBM shall operate on a pass-through pricing model. The amount billed to Plan Sponsor for each claim shall equal the actual amount reimbursed to the dispensing pharmacy plus a transparent, pre-agreed administrative fee. PBM shall not retain any spread between the plan-billed amount and pharmacy reimbursement." Why: This provision authorizes undisclosed spread retention and makes it impossible for the employer to validate drug acquisition cost. A pass-through model removes hidden margin and improves pricing transparency. Source: Gold-standard state PBM contract language or ERISA best practice Section 3.2 — Formulary Management HIGH IMPACT Current Language (Remove) "PBM shall have sole and exclusive discretion to add, remove, or reclassify drugs on the Formulary, including but not limited to changes in tier placement, prior authorization requirements, step therapy protocols, and quantity limits. PBM shall provide Plan Sponsor with thirty (30) days' notice of material formulary changes, provided that PBM may implement immediate changes when required by clinical safety considerations, drug recalls, or market withdrawals. Plan Sponsor acknowledges that formulary decisions are informed by PBM's Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee and manufacturer rebate agreements." Suggested Language (Add) "PBM shall not make mid-year changes to formulary tier placement without prior written approval of Plan Sponsor. All formulary changes must be accompanied by clinical justification and 90-day advance notice. PBM shall not base formulary placement primarily on manufacturer rebate agreements where a therapeutically equivalent lower-net-cost alternative is available to the Plan." Why: The current clause gives PBM unilateral control and ties decisions to rebates. Employer approval, longer notice, and lower-net-cost language materially reduce rebate-driven formulary steering. Source: Gold-standard state PBM contract language or ERISA best practice Section 4.2 — Generic MAC Pricing HIGH IMPACT Current Language (Remove) "PBM maintains proprietary MAC pricing lists that are updated periodically based on market conditions. The specific MAC source, methodology, and update frequency are considered PBM trade secrets and are not subject to disclosure." Suggested Language (Add) "PBM shall disclose the MAC source, methodology, and update frequency, and shall provide an appeal process for pharmacies and Plan Sponsor when a MAC price is materially above market. MAC updates shall be made no more frequently than monthly unless mutually agreed." Why: Without transparency or appeals, the employer cannot tell whether MAC pricing is market-based or used to preserve hidden PBM spread. Source: Gold-standard state PBM contract language or ERISA best practice Section 4.3 — Specialty Drugs HIGH IMPACT Current Language (Remove) "Specialty Drug claims shall be priced at AWP minus twelve percent (AWP - 12%) and shall be dispensed exclusively through PharmaFirst Specialty Pharmacy, LLC, PBM's affiliated specialty pharmacy." Suggested Language (Add) "Plan Sponsor retains the right to designate or approve the specialty pharmacy vendor(s) used for dispensing specialty medications. PBM shall provide transparent pricing for specialty drugs including acquisition cost, dispensing fees, and any channel-specific markups." Why: Exclusive channel control prevents competition and hides specialty economics. Employer vendor optionality and transparent pricing are essential for managing high-cost specialty spend. Shorter notice and no penalty preserve contracting leverage and transition flexibility.
